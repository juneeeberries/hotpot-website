    • Food Preparation

    Preparing raw food for hotpot involves selecting a variety of fresh ingredients and arranging them for easy cooking in a communal pot of simmering broth. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to prepare raw food for hotpot:

    Choose Your Ingredients

    Select a variety of raw ingredients based on personal preferences and dietary restrictions. Common choices include thinly sliced meats (beef, lamb, chicken), seafood (shrimp, fish balls, squid), tofu (regular tofu, tofu skin), vegetables (cabbage, spinach, mushrooms, bok choy), noodles (udon, glass noodles), and dumplings.

    Slice and Prepare Ingredients

    Slice meats thinly for quick cooking. Cut vegetables into bite-sized pieces or slices. Prepare seafood by cleaning and deveining shrimp, slicing fish into manageable pieces, and ensuring squid is cleaned and sliced.

    Arrange Ingredients

    Arrange the prepared raw ingredients on serving platters or plates, separating different types for easy access. You can organize them by type or protein source to make it convenient for diners to choose what they want to cook.

    Marinate Meats (Optional)

    Consider marinating meats with sauces or seasonings for added flavor. Common marinades include soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and chili paste. Allow meats to marinate for at least 15-30 minutes before serving.
